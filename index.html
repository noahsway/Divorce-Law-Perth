What to Expect from the Process?

The divorce process will generally start with the filing of a divorce application. Once this is done, the other party will be given time to respond. If they don't respond within the set timeframe, then a default judgment may be issued in your favour.

After that, you will need to attend a mediation session where both parties will try to agree on the division of assets and debts and custody and visitation arrangements for the children. If you can't agree, the matter will go to trial.

During the trial, both sides will present their case before a judge, who will decide who gets what. This is why it's important to have a divorce lawyer in Perth who can help you build a strong case and increase your chances of getting a favourable outcome.

After the judge has decided, the divorce will be finalized, and you will both be free to move on with your lives.

The Role of Family Lawyers in Perth

The family law system is designed to protect the child's best interests. In a divorce, the children are often caught in the middle. They might feel like they have to choose between their parents. It's why both parties must have a good relationship with their lawyers.

A lawyer well-versed in family law will be able to help you understand your rights and obligations as a parent. They can also help you navigate the divorce process, making it less stressful for you and your children.

It's also the lawyer's job to make sure that the child's best interests are taken into account when it comes to decisions about custody and visitation arrangements. They'll assist you in developing a parenting plan that is fair to both parties and considers the children's needs.

Another aspect of divorce that lawyers are so valuable for is the division of assets and debts. This can be a complicated process, especially if there are many. The lawyer will help you understand your rights and devise a fair way to divide everything between you and your spouse.
